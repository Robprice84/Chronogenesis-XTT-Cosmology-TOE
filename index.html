What Is Chronogenesis?

       

            Chronogenesis is a new theory that suggests time isn't just a backdrop for events, but a fundamental, physical field that builds the universe. Imagine time like a fabric that can stretch, compress, and ripple, much like how a curved surface affects objects rolling on it. In Chronogenesis, these twists and curves in the time field are the source of everything we see: mass, energy, gravity, and even the strange rules of quantum mechanics.        

       

            This idea began with a puzzle: observations from telescopes like JWST showing galaxies that formed surprisingly early in the universe's history. Standard models struggled to explain this. Chronogenesis proposes that time itself expanded in the early universe, giving these galaxies more "internal time" to develop, resolving this cosmic mystery without needing hypothetical concepts like dark energy or inflation.        

       

Why Chronogenesis Matters

       

            For decades, physics has sought a single theory to unify the tiny world of quantum mechanics with the vastness of gravity and cosmology. Chronogenesis offers a potential path to this "Theory of Everything" by grounding all fundamental phenomena in the geometry and dynamics of the time field. It provides a unified framework that connects particle physics, the structure of the universe, and the nature of reality itself.
